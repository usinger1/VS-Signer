VS-Singer: Vision-Guided Stereo Singing Voice Synthesis with Consistency Schrödinger Bridge


Submitted to ICASSP 2025


1. Abstract

In this work, an innovative framework, referred to as VS-Singer, is proposed to incorporate both the capability of visual acoustic matching and stereo singing voice synthesizing. Specifically, VS-Singer consists of a modal interaction network, a decoder based on consistency Schrödinger bridge and a spatial-aware feature enhancement module. The modal interaction network is to fuses spatial features with text encoding to produce hidden sequences. Besides, to increase sampling speed, we propose the consistency Schrödinger bridge, which establish a tractable diffusion bridge between the hidden sequence and binaural mel-spectrograms, enabling to generate samples in only one step. To the best of our knowledge, this work is the first to integrate visual acoustic matching with stereo singing voice synthesis into a unified framework. Experimental results show that VS-Singer can efficiently generate stereo singing voice that matches the scene perspective in one step, particularly achieving a 2x inference speed-up over the comparative cascaded model.
